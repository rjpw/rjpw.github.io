Unfortunately, to borrow a webbish idiom, crazy list of links is crazy. What we have here is hardly more than a loosely categorized bookmark collection (not originally for public consumption) drawn from a sense-making exercise lasting several months. I have been on a mission to make some sense
of the current state of HTML5 application development. This search is mostly from the perspective of client side
web applications (single-page apps), particularly those that support custom visualizations.

A cursory examination of this list will reveal that there are quite
a number of missing libraries, frameworks, and techniques that any reasonably seasoned developer might have expected me to include. I may include such frameworks eventually, if only for their comparative value, but that wasn't the original intention of this list. I was following something of a depth-first search that took me into the joys of *immediate-mode* approaches.

As lists go, please don't be surprised if the categories grow or change, or if one of these links moves to a new spot.

In case that isn't clear, here is an explicit caveat: this list is not intended to span the entire space of web client application development frameworks, but is rather a personal exploration of a subspace. I do hope that this exploration will ultimately produce something of a recommendation.

Over the course of the next few weeks I will be writing about the conceptual starting point of this search, the major themes explored, and some potential insights that might make my future development work a little easier.
